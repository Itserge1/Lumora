# 📋 Guide to Medical Laboratory Report Types

This repository contains a structured overview of the different types of medical laboratory reports commonly encountered
in healthcare. While specific tests vary by facility, most reports generally fall into one of the six major categories
outlined below.

## 1. Hematology (Blood Cells)

**Focus:** Analysis of the cells within the blood. These reports are typically quantitative (counts and percentages).

* **CBC (Complete Blood Count):** Counts Red Blood Cells, White Blood Cells, and Platelets.
* **Coagulation Panels (PT/INR, PTT):** Measures blood clotting time (essential for patients on blood thinners).
* **ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate):** A non-specific test for inflammation in the body.

## 2. Clinical Chemistry (Metabolism)

**Focus:** Analysis of the chemical components in blood plasma to assess organ function. This is the broadest category
of testing.

* **BMP/CMP (Metabolic Panels):** Evaluates Kidney function (Creatinine, BUN), Liver function (ALT, AST), Electrolytes (
  Sodium, Potassium), and Glucose.
* **Lipid Panel:** Measures cholesterol levels (HDL, LDL, Triglycerides).
* **Thyroid Panel:** Measures TSH, T3, and T4 hormones.
* **HbA1c:** Measures average blood sugar over the past 3 months.

## 3. Microbiology (Infections)

**Focus:** Identification of pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites). These results are often qualitative (
Positive/Negative).

* **Cultures:** Growing organisms from samples (e.g., Urine Culture, Throat Culture, Blood Culture).
* **Sensitivity Reports:** Lists which antibiotics are effective against a discovered bacteria.
* **Viral Panels:** PCR or antigen testing for viruses like Influenza, COVID-19, or HIV.

## 4. Pathology & Cytology (Tissues)

**Focus:** Microscopic examination of tissue or cellular samples. These reports are narrative/descriptive rather than
numerical.

* **Biopsy Report:** Detailed analysis of tissue removed from the body (e.g., moles, lumps, polyps).
* **Pap Smear:** Screening of cervical cells for abnormalities.
* **Surgical Pathology:** Examination of organs or tumors removed during surgery.

## 5. Urinalysis

**Focus:** Physical, chemical, and microscopic examination of urine.

* **Routine Urinalysis (UA):** Screens for blood, protein, glucose, ketones, and bacteria.
* **24-Hour Urine Collection:** Aggregated analysis to test kidney filtration rates or specific hormone secretion.

## 6. Immunology & Serology

**Focus:** Analysis of the immune system's response, specifically looking for antibodies or antigens.

* **Autoimmune Panels:** ANA tests (Antinuclear Antibodies) for conditions like Lupus or Rheumatoid Arthritis.
* **Allergy Testing:** Measurement of IgE antibodies for specific allergens.
* **Blood Typing:** Identification of blood group (A, B, AB, O) and Rh factor.
